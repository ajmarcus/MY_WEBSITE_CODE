Dot Mountain Series

Dot production is about time and listening. In 2020, our conception of how much time we “have” and the rate at which it passes have gone through a metamorphosis. In the wake of a global pandemic and the murder of George Floyd, we are called to listen more carefully to the pain that surrounds us and to the stories of people outside our solitary realms. Dot making keeps my restless hands moving while I listen to the world change around me and watch myself grow in the face of it. The undulating texture that emerges from hours of stippling over acrylic strokes is ceaselessly surprising. This act of covering achieves a transformation rather than an erasure of the underlying surface. It gives me hope that with many miniscule gestures, we can emerge positively from this time of struggle with new insight and definitive movement in new directions.
